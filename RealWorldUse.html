Use Of X-Rays

X-rays are created in machines by passing a current between two electrodes in an ‘evacuated tube’. As the electrons hit the positive electrode, X-rays are generated. X-rays are commonly used to photograph broken bones in hospitals but are also used in CAT scans and fluoroscopy.

Before the use of gamma rays, a high-energy, concentrated dose of X-rays was used to treat some cancers. X-rays are still used to identify tumours.
